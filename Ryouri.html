About Us: Our Story

"Ryouri's mission is to bring healthy, authentic, and delicious Japanese cuisine to the world."

Founded in 2012 by well-respected Japanese-American Chef George Hitsugaya, Ryouri started as one small noodle shop on a backroad in Detroit, Michigan. The cuisine developed a cult following in the area, due to its umami flavors and reasonable prices. Hitsugaya himself, also became popular for entertaining customers with Japanese legends and anecdotes.

Ryouri was no longer simply a restaurant, but a hub of cultural activity within the neighborhood. Community members organized culture workshops, story nights for kids, and numerous other events to get people involved and interested in global society.

Although Hitsugaya passed in 2014, his legacy of merging authentic Japanese culture with cuisine continues to this day, allowing people the world-over to join our Global Village.

"Good food starts with a story. There is a history behind every dish; my job, as a chef, is to extract that history and serve it to my customers alongside the dish. This way, they can have a true appreciation for the meal, and what it represents. I think, once you engage with food in this way... it changes everything."

- George Hitsugaya, Ryouri Founder and Head Chef (1945-2013)
